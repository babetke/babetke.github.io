Before coming to the University of Texas at Austin for my PhD, I recieved my bachelor's degree in Natural Resources (emphasis on Wildlife Conservation and Management) with a minor in Verterinary Science at the University of Arizona. As an undergraduate, I participated in the Undergraduate Biological Research Program (UBRP) and the Doris Duke Conservation Scholar Program (DDCSP). I was the lab technician of the [Wildlife Endocrinology Lab](https://cals.arizona.edu/research/ecology/webpages/people/people.html) and an intern at the [Laboratory for the Evolutionary Endocrinology of Primates (LEEP)](https://leepanthropology.weebly.com/people.html). My undergraduate research investigated the correlation between stress levels determined by fecal glucocorticoids and forage quality in Sonoran pronghorn, supervised by David Christianson. While I was in the conservation scholar program, I interned at the California Department of Wildlife's investagtions lab, known as the Wildlife Investigations Lab (WIL). Learning about the human impacts of wildlife health propelled me towards a graduate career in disease ecology!
