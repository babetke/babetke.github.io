**Social Vulnerability and COVID-19 Related Hospitalizations in Austin, Tx**

Severe health outcomes such as hospitalization appear to be heterogeneous in the US and in Austin, Tx. It is important to understand what factors are associated with severe outcome. Recent work had highlighted comorbities such as old age and heart condition. However, sociodemographic variables and metrics of social vulnerability could have an impact on severe heath outcomes. We use the SVI developed by the CDC and its components, obtained from the 2018 ACS, to identify significant linear predictors of COVID-19 hospitalizations per zip code tabular area (ZCTA).

**Sociodemographic and Ecological Associations with Murine Typhus in Texas, USA** 

Murine typhus is a fleaborne disease endemic to California and Texas. In recent years, an increase in cases and geographic area have been noticed in Texas. It is important to understand what factors are associated with human case incidence as it could inform public health policy. In addition drivers of fleaborne disease, Rickettsioses specifically, are poorly understood. To understand if human infection incidence (number of cases per 100,000) of Murine typhus in 2018 -2014 differs between urban and rural dominated counties in Texas, I am conducting a county level regression analysis with county classification (urban and rural) and additional covariates (Precipitation, Average temperature, Minimum Temperature, Maximum Temperature, and Vegetation cover.

**Zoonotic Pathogen Prevalence in the Virginia Opossum in Urban and Rural habitats in Central Texas**

The Virginia opossum (*Didelphis virginiana*) is a generalist and found in both urban and rural landscapes. They serve as bridges for several zoonotic pathogens such as Murine typhus and Chagas disease that are present in Texas. However, landscape and demographic associations of pathogen prevalence in urban adapted mammals, opossums especially, are not well understood. I am collecting blood and ectoparasites of opossums that are brought to Austin Wildlife Rescue starting the summer of 2021. The samples will be tested for presence of *Rickettsia typhi* and *Trypanosoma cruzi* using PCR and IFA techniques*.* Statistical analysis will be conducted to see if pathogen infection differs between urban and rural habitats as well as host demography.
